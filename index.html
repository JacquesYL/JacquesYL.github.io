About Me

Yueliang (Jacques) Lu is a 3rd-year Ph.D. Student in Finance at the Belk College of Business at University of North Carolina at Charlotte. His research interests revolve around asset pricing, risk management, derivatives, and machine learning in finance . His current work focus on anomalies in stock and option markets.

Jacques' research has been presented globally and has been recognized with best paper award and research fellowships from international academic societies, including Financial Management Association (winner), INFORMS (finalist), American Finance Assocation (travel grant), and Global Association of Risk Professionals (research fellowship). On August 2020, he obtained his first major publication in the European Journal of Operational Research.

Jacques hold a master's degree in Quantitative Finance and Risk Analytics, a #7 ranked best national quant program, at the Lally School of Management at Rensselaer Polytechnic Institute (RPI) and a bachelor's degree in Finance with a minor in English Language & Literature from Beijing Foreign Studies University in China. During his master study in RPI, he was selected as one of outstanding graduate scholars and is highlighted in the official Admission webpage.
